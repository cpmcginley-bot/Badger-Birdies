About Badger Birdies

Our Story

Badger Birdies was created by two friends and golf enthusiasts — Jason Reetz and Collin McGinley, students at the University of Wisconsin–Madison who share a passion for golf, competition, and community.

When we first came to campus, we quickly realized that finding golf partners or even knowing where to play wasn’t as simple as we thought. Madison and Dane County are home to some of the most beautiful and well-maintained public courses in the Midwest — from Odana Hills and Yahara Hills to Glenway Golf Park — yet there wasn’t a single platform connecting UW students to these amazing local opportunities.

Most students don’t have the luxury of a car, a country club membership, or the time to coordinate tee times. We saw a clear need for something simple — a way for golfers to connect, network, and enjoy the game together without the barriers of transportation, cost, or scheduling headaches.

So we built Badger Birdies.

What We Believe

Badger Birdies was founded on the idea that golf at UW–Madison should be as accessible and social as any campus sport or club. We believe that golf isn’t just about birdies and bogeys — it’s about the friendships and experiences made along the way.

Our mission is to create a community where students can easily find courses, meet other golfers, share their scores, and compete in a friendly, welcoming environment. Whether you’re an experienced player looking for competition or a beginner hoping to learn, Badger Birdies gives you a place to belong.

Our Vision

We envision a platform that brings together the talent, passion, and scenic beauty that define golf in Dane County. With courses set among rolling hills, lakes, and parkland fairways, Madison offers some of the most rewarding golf in the country — yet too few students get to experience it.

Badger Birdies helps solve that. We provide a central hub where students can:

  • Discover affordable, nearby golf courses.
  • Connect with other UW–Madison golfers by handicap or skill level.
  • Track scores and leaderboards to bring friendly competition to campus.
  • Stay updated on local events, deals, and student scrambles.

By making golf more accessible, we hope to grow a vibrant golf culture at UW–Madison — one built on inclusion, sportsmanship, and school pride.

Why It Matters

Golf has a unique way of bringing people together. It teaches patience, respect, and perseverance — qualities that carry far beyond the course. For students balancing classes, jobs, and life, a day on the course can be both a challenge and a reset.

Badger Birdies exists to make those experiences possible. To give UW–Madison students a break from the rush, to build friendships through competition, and to celebrate the sport we all love.

The Future of Badger Birdies

What started as a small idea between two golfers has grown into something bigger than we imagined. As we continue developing the site, our goal is to expand features like matchmaking, score tracking, and course reviews, while keeping the platform 100% student-focused and easy to use.

We’re proud to represent the Badger spirit — resilient, passionate, and community-driven — both on and off the course.

Because at the end of the day, golf at UW–Madison isn’t just about playing well. It’s about playing together.
